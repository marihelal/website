Current Research

Developing a protocol for hand-held XRF analysis of carbonates:

I was awarded the Ramsden Research Fellowship for the summer 2025 to work on an independent project under the guidance of my PI, Francis Macdonald, and graduate student Ellery Renger. This project, which I am continuing during the academic year, is to develop a protocol for analyzing carbonate major element compositions using a hand-held XRF instrument, which is cheaper, quicker, and non-destructive as compared to the traditional method of using a mass spectrometer. I will then apply this protocol to analyze the drifts in relevant major elements and isotope ratios of carbonate samples that record the Trezona anomaly, one of the largest recorded CIEs.

Radiative transfer modeling of proto-planetary disks:

As a member of the Bergner lab group and under the guidance of graduate student Jack Anderson, I am working on a project modeling proto-planetary disk spectra using radmc3d. The goal is to test methods for correcting the effect of rotational motion on column density calculations. Accurate column density values are crucial for understanding planetary formation in these disks, so it is necessary to account for phenomena that alter the shape of the spectral peaks of molecules.
